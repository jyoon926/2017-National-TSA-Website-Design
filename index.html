— Privatization of Space Exploration —

In the past and today, there have been and are many ongoing searches for ways to explore our solar system or privatization of space exploration. On October 4, 1957, the Soviets launched the first ever artificial satellite, Sputnik 1, into space. On July 20, 1969, Astronaut Neil Armstrong took “a giant step for mankind” as he stepped onto the moon. Six Apollo missions were made to explore the moon between 1969 and 1972. On November 13, 1971, the Mariner 9 probe became the first craft to orbit another world, Mars. By the end of the 1970s, the Voyager spacecraft had sent back detailed images of Jupiter and Saturn, their rings, and their moons. Six years later the probe Viking 2 discovered martian frost on the Martian surface. There are many ways to explore the solar system, these are just a few ways the world has privatized space.

— Vehicles and Spacecrafts —

The Evolution of Space Vehicles

Space vehicles first started with the mathematical demonstration about how to achieve liftoff using liquid fuels. This was eventually achieved when Robert Goddard launched the first liquid-fueled rocket on March 16, 1926. Later on nearing the end of World War II, Germany launched ballistic missiles respectively known as the A4 and the V-2. The space age then started when Satellite Sputnik became the first satellite to be launched into space on October 4, 1957.

Manned Spacecrafts

  • The Vostok Program started manned spacecrafts.
  • Mercury was the first US manned spacecraft.​
  • Apollo spacecraft became the first to be able to reach the moon.
  • The Space Shuttle was developed to build Space Stations such as the ISS.
  • China developed the Shenzhou spacecraft becoming the third nation to achieve independent human spaceflight capabilities.
  • NASA is currently developing spacecraft capable of going to Mars, building the Orion spacecraft launched by the Space Launch System.
  • Current Programs are: Soyuz Program, Shenzhou Program, ISS, Tiangong-2.

Unmanned Spacecrafts

  • Sputnik 1 and 2 became the first unmanned spacecrafts (Satellites).
  • Explorer 1 was the first US unmanned spacecraft (Satellite).
  • There were many other satellites that were launched.
  • Deep Space satellites were launched.
  • Mars Rover spacecraft was launched.
